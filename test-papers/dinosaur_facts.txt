Dinosaurs ruled the Earth for over 160 million years, making them one of the most successful groups of animals in history. Some were gentle plant-eaters with long necks or armored bodies, while others were fast hunters with sharp teeth and claws. Scientists study fossils like bones, footprints, eggs, and even feathers to learn how dinosaurs moved, ate, and survived. Their world was filled with forests, rivers, volcanoes, and changing climates, making the age of dinosaurs one of the most fascinating periods in Earth’s history.
